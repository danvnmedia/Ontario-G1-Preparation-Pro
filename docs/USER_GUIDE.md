# User Guide - Ontario G1 Preparation Pro

Welcome to Ontario G1 Preparation Pro! This guide will help you make the most of our practice test platform.

## Getting Started

### What is the G1 Test?

The G1 test is the first step to getting your driver's license in Ontario. It consists of:
- 40 multiple-choice questions
- Two sections: Traffic Signs (20 questions) and Rules of the Road (20 questions)
- You must score 16/20 (80%) on each section to pass
- No time limit (but typically takes 30-45 minutes)

### Using This Application

Our application helps you prepare by providing:
- Practice quizzes similar to the real test
- Instant feedback on your answers
- Detailed explanations for each question
- Study materials covering all test topics

## Tips for Success

### Before You Start

1. **Read the Official Handbook**
   - Download from: https://www.ontario.ca/document/official-mto-drivers-handbook
   - Read all sections thoroughly
   - Pay special attention to signs and signals

2. **Set a Study Schedule**
   - Study 30-60 minutes daily
   - Focus on one topic at a time
   - Review regularly

3. **Understand Don't Memorize**
   - Learn the reasoning behind rules
   - Understand why certain laws exist
   - Think about real-world application

### During Practice

1. **Take Your Time**
   - Read questions carefully
   - Don't rush
   - Consider all options before answering

2. **Use Explanations**
   - Read explanations for both correct and incorrect answers
   - Understand why each answer is right or wrong

3. **Track Your Progress**
   - Note areas where you struggle
   - Focus extra study time on weak areas
   - Retake quizzes until you consistently score 80%+

4. **Simulate Test Conditions**
   - Find a quiet place
   - Minimize distractions
   - Take full practice tests
   - Time yourself (though the real test isn't timed)

### Study Strategies

#### For Road Signs
- Learn signs by category (warning, regulatory, information)
- Pay attention to shapes and colors
- Understand the meaning, not just memorize
- Practice with the Road Signs Reference section

#### For Rules and Regulations
- Focus on numbers (speed limits, distances, times)
- Understand right-of-way scenarios
- Learn parking rules and restrictions
- Study G1 license restrictions carefully

#### For Safety Topics
- Learn about impaired driving laws (zero tolerance for G1)
- Understand seatbelt requirements
- Know the rules about distractions and cell phones
- Study emergency procedures

## Common Topics to Master

### Must-Know Numbers
- Speed limits in different zones (school, residential, highway)
- Blood alcohol limits (0.00% for G1 drivers)
- Following distances (2-3 seconds)
- Parking distances (3m from fire hydrant, 9m from intersection)
- Demerit points for violations

### G1 License Restrictions
- Must be accompanied by licensed driver (4+ years experience)
- Cannot drive between midnight and 5 AM
- Zero blood alcohol level
- No 400-series highways
- No handheld devices

### Critical Rules
- Right-of-way at intersections
- Passing rules and no-passing zones
- Traffic light meanings (including advanced green)
- Railroad crossing procedures
- Emergency vehicle protocols

## Taking the Real Test

### Before the Test
- Book your test at: https://drivetest.ca/
- Bring required identification
- Arrive 30 minutes early
- Get a good night's sleep
- Eat a healthy meal

### During the Test
- Stay calm and focused
- Read each question carefully
- Use process of elimination
- Don't rush - there's no time limit
- Review answers if allowed

### After the Test
- If you pass: Congratulations! You'll receive your G1 license
- If you don't pass: You can retake after waiting period (check DriveTest website)
- Keep studying and practicing
